Have you ever heard of SAS? Student Activity Station, in short "SAS" is a student organization at APU that organizes local cultural exchange events. Through the impactful projects that involve both APU students and Beppu citizens, they get to learn about different unique cultures and make new international friends. Let's get to know the experience of new member Baasankhuu Sainzaya who joined from this spring semester (see sidebar.)

Multicultural Quest

In Multicultural Quest, we invited APU students to learn more about the place they are living in, Oita. Many students came for the event and enjoyed one day together, exploring Oita through various activities at our campus. The event was free for everyone so participants can enjoy the event despite any financial worries.

Beppu Trails

Although our team has helped other teams with couple of other events, Beppu Trails was the last event of the semester. Therefore, we put in extra effort and learned many meaningful lessons from organizing this event. In the event, we scrolled through Beppu especially popular areas such as Kannawa, Beppu Eki and a must-visit "Beppu Tower".

My experience at SAS

As a recipient of MEXT scholarship, it was one of my missions to connect different cultures and promote Japan's marvellous culture and tradition to others. With this goal and personal interest and aspiration, I decided to apply to SAS, and luckily got accepted. Ever since becoming SAS member, I am learning a lot from Japan's business etiquettes and organizing cultural events to creating a professional poster that attracts participants.
